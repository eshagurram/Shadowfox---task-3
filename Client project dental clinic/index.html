Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can medication completely relieve tooth pain?
Not entirely. While medications may offer short-term relief, long-lasting relief requires identifying and treating the root cause through professional dental care.
2. Does teeth scaling cause enamel damage, sensitivity, or gaps?
No. Scaling safely removes plaque and tartar and does not damage enamel or create gaps. Temporary sensitivity may occur but usually subsides.
3. Can fluorosis stains be removed through scaling?
Scaling removes surface stains but not fluorosis. Treatments like bleaching, veneers, or crowns may be required.
4. Can painful teeth be treated with fillings?
Not always. If the cavity reaches the pulp, root canal treatment or extraction may be required.
5. Is a crown necessary after root canal treatment?
Yes. A crown protects the treated tooth and prevents fractures.
6. Can milk teeth need RCT?
Yes. RCT can preserve a milk tooth and prevent infection or early loss.
7. How can I maintain good oral hygiene?
Brush twice daily, rinse after meals, floss regularly, and visit your dentist routinely.
8. What is the best age to get braces?
Usually between 12 and 14 years, though earlier treatment may be required in some cases.
9. Do all wisdom teeth need removal?
No. Only problematic wisdom teeth need removal.
10. Is replacing missing teeth important?
Yes. It maintains bite alignment and chewing function.
11. What are the benefits of dental implants?
They restore appearance, function, and confidence with a natural-looking solution.
12. Which toothbrush and toothpaste should I use?
Use a soft/medium brush and fluoride toothpaste. Replace every 3 months.
13. What causes tooth sensitivity?
Enamel wear, decay, exposed roots, or cracks in teeth.
14. How often should I see a dentist?
Every six months for routine check-ups and cleaning.
